## 2. Methodology Summary

Three independent estimation methods were applied and triangulated:

- **Method A (Top-Down)**: Starting from the broadest credible market figure (global orthobiologics, ~$6.3B in 2024), applying segment filters to isolate cartilage-specific biologics and cell therapies.

### Why the Estimates Converge at ~$700M–$800M

All three methods arrive at a similar range for the cell therapy and biologics-specific segment when scope is consistently defined. The main sources of divergence are:

1. **Scope creep in industry reports**: Many cited market figures ($1.5B–$5.98B range for "cartilage repair") include mechanical procedures, viscosupplementation, or broader orthobiologic categories. When constrained to cell/biologic products for articular cartilage, the number compresses to $700M–$1B.

2. **Vericel's dominance in the US**: MACI generates $197M–$240M in a US market estimated at $350–400M for cell-based approaches. At 50–60% US share, that implies a global market of ~$580–$700M for ACI/MACI-class products alone.

3. **Geographic skew**: The global market is disproportionately weighted toward North America (~51.5%) given US reimbursement coverage, Vericel's dedicated commercial infrastructure, and FDA-approved MACI. Europe and Asia are underpenetrated relative to disease burden.

**Reconciled 2025 baseline for primary market (cell therapy and biologics, articular cartilage)**: **$750M–$800M**

## 9. TAM / SAM / SOM Estimates

### 9.1 Definitions Applied

- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market): Revenue opportunity if all patients with articular cartilage defects that could benefit from biologic/cell-based repair received best-available therapy globally
- **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market): Portion of TAM reachable given current regulatory approvals, reimbursement access, and physician adoption in accessible geographies
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market): Realistic near-term market capture for a new entrant or existing player with a differentiated biologic/cell therapy product

---

### 9.2 TAM — Total Addressable Market

**Definition**: Full theoretical opportunity — what the global cell therapy and biologics cartilage market could be if current treatment barriers were resolved.

**TAM Build (2025)**:

Global knee OA prevalence: 374.7M cases ([PLOS One](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0320115))
Of these, ~15–20% have articular cartilage defects that are candidates for repair (excluding end-stage OA requiring TKA):
→ 374.7M × 17.5% = **65.6M candidacy pool** for cartilage repair globally

Subset that would benefit from biologic/cell-based approach (Grade III–IV focal defects, younger patients, failed conservative): ~5–8% of OA pool:
→ 374.7M × 6.5% = **24.4M target patients globally**

At an average biologic treatment revenue of $8,000–$15,000 per patient episode (below full ACI ASP, reflecting lower-cost biologics, growth factors, and future price normalization):
→ 24.4M × $10,000 = **$244B theoretical maximum** (clearly not actualized in any near-term scenario)

This illustrates why TAM in this context is better defined as the realistic addressable market, not the theoretical maximum. A more practical TAM bounds the opportunity:

**Practical TAM (2025)**: The total global market for articular cartilage repair — all modalities including biologic, surgical, and mechanical — in addressable geographies with functioning healthcare systems capable of offering these procedures:

→ Total cartilage repair market all modalities: **$1.73B** (Mordor Intelligence, 2025 baseline)
→ Biologic/cell-therapy portion if adoption expanded to fully capture potential (assuming regulatory and reimbursement success): **$2.0B–$2.5B** [PROXY — assumes biologic share grows from 60.5% to 70–75% over 5 years as cell therapies displace mechanical techniques]

**TAM 2025 (Base Case)**: **$1.6B–$2.0B** (total articular cartilage repair, biologic-expandable universe)
**TAM 2025 (Primary Segment — cell/biologic only)**: **$1.0B–$1.2B** [PROXY — applying 62% biologic share to expanded addressable market]

| Scenario | TAM 2025 |
|---|---|
| Bear | $1.4B (conservative biologic adoption, strict scope) |
| Base | $1.7B (total cartilage repair, all modalities) |
| Bull | $2.2B (including adjacent biologic applications in cartilage) |

### 9.4 SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market

**Definition**: Realistic near-term market capture for a new entrant with a differentiated cell therapy or biologic product (allogeneic, off-the-shelf, next-generation), or existing player looking to expand.

**SOM Assumptions**:
- New entrant (allogeneic/off-the-shelf product): Could realistically capture 3–8% of SAM within 5 years of commercial launch

### 10.2 Growth Driver Analysis

**Driver 1 — Aging Population and OA Epidemic**
- Global OA prevalence: 607M in 2021, growing due to aging and obesity ([Frontiers/GBD 2021](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/medicine/articles/10.3389/fmed.2024.1476853/full))
- Global 60+ population: doubling to 2.1B by 2050 ([WHO](https://www.who.gov/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/ageing-and-health))
- OA prevalence increase: 132.2% from 1990 to 2022 ([Frontiers/GBD](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/medicine/articles/10.3389/fmed.2024.1476853/full))
- **Impact**: Steady structural demand driver; expands the patient pool by approximately 2–3% per year through 2035

**Driver 2 — Obesity as Accelerating Risk Factor**
- 2.5 billion adults overweight globally (WHO, 2022); obesity rates double since 1990 ([The Lancet 2025 study](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(25)00355-1/fulltext))
- High BMI contributes 20.4% of OA burden ([PMC/GBD](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11602326/))
- **Impact**: Accelerates cartilage degeneration, particularly in younger patients (creating demand for non-arthroplasty solutions)

**Driver 3 — Procedure Innovation (MACI Arthro)**
- FDA cleared MACI Arthro in August 2024 — first arthroscopic delivery of ACI, reducing procedure invasiveness ([noted in search results from Vericel IR](https://investors.vcel.com/news-releases/news-release-details/vericel-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2024-financial))
- Arthroscopic delivery expands addressable surgeon base and reduces recovery time, increasing adoption
- **Impact**: Estimated 15–25% near-term volume acceleration for MACI in US market; similar trajectory expected if MACI Arthro pursues ex-US regulatory approval

**Driver 4 — Reimbursement Expansion**
- US: 90% of MACI cases approved on initial submission in 2024 ([MACI HCP site](https://www.maci.com/healthcare-professionals/practice-resources/))
- Germany: MACI reimbursed for Grade III–IV defects ([MTR Consult](https://mtrconsult.com/news/matrix-associated-autologous-chondrocyte-implantation-knee-joint-reimbursed-germany-0))
- **Impact**: Expanding reimbursement in Europe and Asia represents a meaningful growth lever; each major payer system that covers ACI unlocks a multi-million-dollar incremental market

**Driver 5 — Next-Generation Cell Therapies (Allogeneic and iPSC-Derived)**
- Autologous ACI requires two surgeries; allogeneic/off-the-shelf products (iPSC-derived chondrocytes, MSC products) could eliminate this barrier
- iPSC-derived clinical trials advancing: [Science Advances (clinical-grade iPSC chondrogenic micropellets)](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.adw4911); [Nature Regenerative Medicine (long-term iPSC cartilage repair)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41536-025-00447-6)
- MSC clinical trial evidence: randomized trials showing 65% defect filling vs. 40% with microfracture ([MDPI/IJMS](https://www.mdpi.com/1422-0067/24/12/9939))
- **Impact**: If allogeneic products achieve approval by 2028–2030, market expansion could be significant (3–5x market size vs. autologous-only scenario, per analogous cell therapy market expansions)

**Driver 6 — Sports Injury and Athletic Population**
- Up to 36–38% prevalence of cartilage lesions in high-level athletes ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4297068/))
- 900,000 Americans affected by cartilage injuries annually ([PMC systematic review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9251824/))
- Sports medicine remains the dominant channel for cartilage repair procedures
- **Impact**: Relatively stable but growing addressable pool; sports-active aging population (55–70 year range) increasingly demanding repair over replacement

**Driver 7 — Asia-Pacific Infrastructure and Income Growth**
- Asia-Pacific projected at 15.64% CAGR for cartilage repair ([iHealthcare Analyst](https://www.ihealthcareanalyst.com/knee-cartilage-repair-market/))
- Rising healthcare infrastructure investment in China, India, Southeast Asia
- **Impact**: Asia-Pacific could represent 15–20% of global market by 2030 vs. current ~12–15%
